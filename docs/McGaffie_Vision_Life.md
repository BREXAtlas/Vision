## PART ONE: WHERE THE MONEY ACTUALLY LIVES

Before the day begins, understand the architecture underneath it — because this is what separates people who *look* rich from people who *are* rich.

### The $20M "Minimum Balance" — What That Really Means

That number on your board isn't sitting in a checking account. Nobody wealthy does that (FDIC only insures $250K per depositor per bank — the rest would be unprotected and losing value to inflation). Here's how it's actually structured:

**Private Banking Relationship.** At $10M+ in investable assets, you graduate from regular banking into *private banking* — J.P. Morgan Private Bank, Goldman Sachs Private Wealth, Northern Trust. Your "$20M minimum balance" is your qualifying relationship size. You get a dedicated banker who answers your calls personally, access to private deals, and lending on terms regular customers never see.

**The actual layout of $20M liquid:**
- ~$500K in operating cash (bills, payroll for household staff, daily life)
- ~$5M in a Treasury bill ladder — U.S. government debt maturing every 30–90 days, currently paying interest, backed by the government, no FDIC limit needed
- ~$10M in a diversified portfolio (index funds, dividend stocks, municipal bonds — munis are popular because the interest is federally tax-free)
- ~$4.5M in alternatives — private equity, real estate funds, the deals your private banker brings you

At even 4–5% blended yield, $20M throws off **$800K–$1M per year** without touching the principal. That's the quiet math of wealth: the money makes money faster than you can spend it living well.

### How Billionaires Hold Billions

You were right — it's assets, and specifically it's mostly **equity**. A billionaire's net worth is mostly *ownership of companies*. If BrexAtlas is worth $3B and you own 40%, you're worth $1.2B — but that's stock, not cash.

So how do they spend? **They borrow against the stock.** It's called a securities-backed line of credit. The bank lends you, say, $50M at a low rate using your shares as collateral. Why borrow instead of sell? Selling triggers capital gains tax (20%+ federal). Borrowing triggers nothing. The strategy has a nickname: **"buy, borrow, die"** — buy assets, borrow against them to live, and heirs inherit with a stepped-up cost basis. This is the single most important mechanic to understand about billionaire finance.

### The Family Office

At $100M+, you don't call a bank — you *build one for yourself*. The McGaffie Family Office is a private company with maybe 8–15 employees whose entire job is your family's wealth: a CIO managing investments, a tax director, an estate attorney, a head of security, a travel and lifestyle manager. Every chauffeur, chef, and pilot in this document is technically employed by the family office LLC — clean payroll, clean liability, clean taxes.

### How the Properties Are Owned

You never own the mansion in your own name. The Houston estate is owned by **McGaffie Holdings LLC**, which is owned by **The McGaffie Family Dynasty Trust**. Why the layers? Lawsuit protection (someone slips at your pool, they sue an LLC, not you), privacy (public records show a company name, not yours), and estate planning (the dynasty trust passes wealth to your kids and grandkids while minimizing estate tax — currently 40% on estates above the exemption). Your estate attorney set this up years ago, and it's why your kids will never start from zero.

## PART FOUR: DAILY VISUALIZATION GUIDE

Visualization works not as magic but as *neurological rehearsal* — you're training your brain's filter (the reticular activating system) to notice opportunities matching the picture, and building the identity that makes bold action feel normal.

**The 10-minute morning protocol:**
1. **(2 min) Still the water.** Sit, breathe slow — 4 counts in, 6 out. You can't imprint on a shaking screen.
2. **(4 min) Live one scene.** Don't watch the vision board like a movie — step *inside one scene* in first person. Today: the walk from the Cullinan to the jet. Feel the Houston heat, hear the engines, smell the tarmac. All five senses. Rotate scenes daily.
3. **(2 min) Speak identity, present tense.** "I create my own wealth. I own companies. I am building the McGaffie legacy." Out loud. Your board already uses "I Am" language — correct instinct.
4. **(2 min) Bridge to today.** Ask: *"What would that Lawrence do before noon today?"* Write down ONE action. This is the step most people skip, and it's the entire mechanism. Vision without a same-day action is just daydreaming; vision + action is direction.

**Evening (2 min):** Before sleep, replay the scene once. The mind marinates on the last input of the day.

## PART FIVE: THE BRIDGE — FROM TODAY TO THE BOARD

The picture runs on a sequence. Here's the honest staircase:

**Stage 1 — First Cash Flow (now → $10K/mo).** One business, one offer, real customers. Your path-to-cash focus is exactly right. Every empire on that board started as one person closing one deal.

**Stage 2 — First $1M net worth.** Comes from *owning* something that grows, not from salary. Reinvest profits. Learn the language now: read up on LLCs, S-corps, and basic tax strategy — the wealthy learn the rules of the game early and it compounds.

**Stage 3 — $1M → $10M.** Scale or sell the business. This is where you form your first holding company and hire your first "team member from the future" — probably a great accountant.

**Stage 4 — $10M+.** Private banking opens its doors. Diversify out of the business into the portfolio described in Part One. The money starts working harder than you do.

**Stage 5 — The board.** Equity in valuable companies + a family office + time freedom. The jet is the *last* purchase, not the first — the wealthy buy assets first and toys with the yield.

**This week's assignment:** Do the 10-minute protocol every morning. And every day, take the ONE bridge action it surfaces. The board tells you where you're going; the action is the vehicle.
